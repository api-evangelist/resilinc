<p><span>The EU Battery Regulation (2023/1542) is raising the bar for how organizations manage their battery supply chains. Many companies have already taken an important first step: building multi-tier supply chain visibility, sometimes going as deep as ten tiers.</span><span> </span></p>
<p><span>But here is the key question: </span><i><span>Is your supply chain map enabling compliance, or just visibility?</span></i><span> </span></p>
<p><span> </span></p>
<h2><span>Mapping is the foundation, not the finish line </span></h2>
<p><span>A detailed supply chain map is essential. It tells you who your suppliers are and how materials flow across tiers. With solutions like the Resilinc supply chain graph, organizations can build this visibility using multiple approaches, from autonomous mapping that rapidly scales visibility to validated mapping that delivers highly actionable, decision-ready insights.</span><span> </span></p>
<p><span>However, the regulation does not stop at visibility. It requires companies to act on that information.</span><span> </span></p>
<p><span>To be compliant, organizations need to turn static maps into active, risk-driven systems.</span><span> </span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h2>What comes after mapping?</h2>
<p><span>Once you have visibility, the real work begins. At a high level, compliance under the EU Battery Regulation follows a continuous cycle:</span><span> </span></p>
<p><span>Map </span><span>→</span><span> Assess Risk </span><span>→</span><span> Act </span><span>→</span><span> Verify </span><span>→</span><span> Report </span><span>→</span><span> Repeat</span><span> </span></p>
<p><span>Each step builds on the previous one, turning supply chain visibility into an ongoing, auditable system.</span><span> </span></p>
<p><span>The EU Battery Regulation requires companies to build a structured approach around four key areas:</span><span> </span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h2><span class="TextRun MacChromeBold SCXW219264234 BCX0" lang="EN-US" xml:lang="EN-US"><span class="NormalTextRun SCXW219264234 BCX0">1. Risk identification and assessment</span></span></h2>
<p><span>You need to evaluate risks across your supply chain, especially upstream. This includes:</span><span> </span></p>
<ul>
<li><span>Environmental risks </span><span> </span></li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li><span>Human rights concerns </span><span> </span></li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li><span>Governance issues </span><span> </span></li>
</ul>
<p><span>This is where your supply chain map becomes actionable. Leveraging a dynamic supply chain graph, whether built through autonomous discovery or validated supplier inputs, helps pinpoint where risks are most likely to exist.</span><span> </span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h2><span class="TextRun MacChromeBold SCXW75132651 BCX0" lang="EN-US" xml:lang="EN-US"><span class="NormalTextRun SCXW75132651 BCX0">2. Supplier engagement</span></span></h2>
<p><span>Compliance is not just internal. It extends across your entire supplier network.</span><span> </span></p>
<p><span>You will need to:</span><span> </span></p>
<ul>
<li><span>Communicate expectations clearly </span><span> </span></li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li><span>Collect supplier data and declarations </span><span> </span></li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li><span>Ensure requirements are cascaded beyond Tier 1 </span><span> </span></li>
</ul>
<p><span>Engaging suppliers effectively is critical, especially as regulations require deeper-tier accountability. A connected supply chain graph makes it easier to bring suppliers into the process and maintain alignment across tiers.</span><span> </span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h2><span class="TextRun MacChromeBold SCXW239179938 BCX0" lang="EN-US" xml:lang="EN-US"><span class="NormalTextRun SCXW239179938 BCX0">3. Ongoing monitoring and risk mitigation</span></span><span class="EOP Selected SCXW239179938 BCX0"> </span></h2>
<p><span>Risks are not static, and neither is your supply chain.</span><span> </span></p>
<p><span>Organizations must:</span><span> </span></p>
<ul>
<li><span>Continuously monitor suppliers and regions </span><span> </span></li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li><span>Respond to emerging risks </span><span> </span></li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li><span>Track corrective actions when issues arise </span><span> </span></li>
</ul>
<p><span>This is where many companies struggle. The challenge is moving from one-time assessments to continuous oversight. A living supply chain graph ensures that mapping stays current and usable as conditions change.</span><span> </span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h2><span class="TextRun MacChromeBold SCXW42237186 BCX0" lang="EN-US" xml:lang="EN-US"><span class="NormalTextRun SCXW42237186 BCX0">4. Reporting and transparency</span></span></h2>
<p><span>The regulation requires clear documentation and disclosure of:</span><span> </span></p>
<ul>
<li><span>Due diligence processes </span><span> </span></li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li><span>Identified risks </span><span> </span></li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li><span>Actions taken </span><span> </span></li>
</ul>
<p><span>This information will increasingly feed into requirements like the Battery Passport, making data accuracy and traceability essential. A well-structured supply chain graph supports this by maintaining consistent, traceable records across tiers.</span><span> </span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h2><span class="TextRun MacChromeBold SCXW98885822 BCX0" lang="EN-US" xml:lang="EN-US"><span class="NormalTextRun SCXW98885822 BCX0">Turning compliance into a scalable system</span></span><span class="EOP Selected SCXW98885822 BCX0"> </span></h2>
<p><span>At Resilinc, we see companies facing a common challenge. They have visibility but lack the infrastructure to operationalize it.</span><span> </span></p>
<p><span>A more integrated approach can help:</span><span> </span></p>
<ul>
<li><span>Assessment capabilities allow companies to evaluate supplier risk in a structured, scalable way </span><span> </span></li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li><span>Ongoing monitoring ensures risks are identified as they emerge </span><span> </span></li>
</ul>
<ul>

</ul>
<ul>
<li><b><span>Built-in reporting</span></b><span> simplifies compliance and audit readiness </span><span> </span></li>
</ul>
<p><span>Equally important, suppliers are part of the process. With accessible tools and no-cost entry points, they can contribute data, respond to assessments, and stay aligned with requirements. This makes compliance more collaborative and achievable.</span><span> </span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h2><span class="TextRun MacChromeBold SCXW792218 BCX0" lang="EN-US" xml:lang="EN-US"><span class="NormalTextRun SCXW792218 BCX0">The shift companies need to make</span></span><span class="EOP Selected SCXW792218 BCX0"> </span></h2>
<p><span>The EU Battery Regulation is not just about compliance. It is about accountability across the entire value chain.</span><span> </span></p>
<p><span>Companies that succeed will move beyond static visibility and build living, responsive supply chain systems. Because in the end, it is not about how deep your map goes it is about what you do with it.</span><span> </span></p>
